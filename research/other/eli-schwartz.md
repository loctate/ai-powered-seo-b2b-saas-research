## Source 1 — Control Your AEO with Your Website

* **Published:** March 26, 2026
* **Author:** Eli Schwartz
* **Format:** Newsletter
* **URL:** https://www.productledseo.com/p/websites-are-more-valuable-than-ever
* **Primary topic:** The continued importance of owned websites in answer-engine visibility

### Summary

Eli argues that although AI-generated answers can reduce search traffic, the company website remains an important source of information for answer engines.

The website is the company’s primary controlled source for product facts, positioning, documentation, customer information, and brand narrative.

Rather than abandoning owned content, companies should make the website more customer-focused, accurate, accessible, and useful enough to become a reliable source for both users and AI systems.

### Key Insights

* AI answers do not eliminate the need for a strong website.
* Owned content provides control over product and brand information.
* A useful website increases the likelihood of being used as a source.
* Customer-focused information is more valuable than content written only for algorithms.
* Product facts, evidence, and positioning should be clearly documented.
* Websites remain an important foundation even when discovery occurs elsewhere.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS websites should clearly communicate:

* Target users
* Product capabilities
* Use cases
* Integrations
* Pricing context
* Limitations
* Implementation requirements
* Security and compliance
* Customer outcomes
* Product documentation

## Source 2 — AEO Is Not SEO 2.0

* **Published:** April 9, 2026
* **Author:** Eli Schwartz
* **Format:** Newsletter
* **URL:** https://www.productledseo.com/p/aeo-is-not-seo-20
* **Primary topic:** Differences between traditional SEO and answer-engine optimization

### Summary

Eli warns against assuming that an LLM answer is simply a modern search-results page.

Traditional SEO frequently focuses on helping users discover and select a webpage. AEO can involve a different journey in which the AI system synthesizes information and provides an answer without requiring the user to visit the original sources.

Therefore, copying conventional keyword and ranking tactics into AI-search workflows can lead companies to optimize for a customer journey that may not exist.

### Key Insights

* AEO should not be treated as a direct replacement for traditional SEO.
* AI-assisted user journeys differ from ranked search journeys.
* Keyword rankings may not represent AI recommendation visibility.
* The final objective may be brand inclusion or recommendation rather than a webpage click.
* Companies should begin with customer behavior and business outcomes.
* New terminology should not hide the need for strategic clarity.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

A B2B SaaS team should distinguish between:

* Creating demand
* Educating the market
* Generating website traffic
* Being included in AI-generated shortlists
* Supporting product evaluation
* Influencing purchase decisions
* Creating pipeline and revenue

Each objective may require different content, channels, and measurement methods.

## Source 3 — SEO Is a Product, AEO Is Brand

* **Published:** May 14, 2026
* **Author:** Eli Schwartz
* **Format:** Newsletter
* **URL:** https://www.productledseo.com/p/seo-is-a-product-aeo-is-brand
* **Primary topic:** Organizational ownership of SEO, AEO, product, engineering, and brand visibility

### Summary

Eli argues that AI-search visibility should not automatically become the sole responsibility of the SEO team.

How an AI system describes a company depends on a wide range of signals, including product experience, engineering, brand, public relations, customer sentiment, third-party references, and company-owned information.

An SEO team placed entirely inside marketing may lack the authority to implement technical, product, or organizational changes. Therefore, AI visibility requires wider cross-functional responsibility.

### Key Insights

* AEO is strongly connected to brand perception.
* SEO and AEO often require work outside the marketing department.
* Product and engineering teams influence technical discoverability.
* PR and brand teams influence how the company is represented externally.
* Customer experience influences public discussions and recommendations.
* Organizational structure can limit execution even when the strategy is correct.
* AI-search responsibility should be distributed across relevant teams.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

A B2B SaaS AI-search program may require participation from:

* SEO
* Content
* Product marketing
* Product management
* Engineering
* Public relations
* Customer success
* Sales
* Legal and compliance
* Executive leadership

## Consolidated Research Takeaways

Eli Schwartz’s work suggests that B2B SaaS teams should:

* Avoid treating AEO as keyword SEO with a new name
* Begin with real user journeys and business objectives
* Maintain a strong and authoritative company website
* Document product information clearly
* Treat AI visibility as both a product and brand issue
* Assign responsibility across multiple departments
* Measure recommendation and business influence, not only traffic
* Protect the accuracy of the company’s brand and product narrative

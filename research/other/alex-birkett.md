## Source 1 — AI Search FAQs

* **Published:** April 16, 2026
* **Author:** Alex Birkett
* **Format:** Article
* **URL:** `https://beomniscient.com/blog/ai-search-faqs/`
* **Primary topic:** Common misconceptions, measurement challenges, and practical AI-search strategy

### Summary

Alex describes two unhelpful extremes in the AI-search discussion.

One extreme assumes that nothing has changed and that companies should simply continue traditional SEO. The other assumes that everything has changed and that organizations should abandon established practices in favor of every new AEO or GEO tactic.

The article recommends a more balanced approach. Companies should retain effective SEO, content, brand, and distribution practices while testing new AI-search methods with clear hypotheses and measurable business objectives.

### Key Insights

* AI search is neither completely new nor identical to traditional SEO.
* Established SEO and content practices remain useful.
* New AI-search tactics should be tested rather than accepted uncritically.
* Visibility metrics do not automatically represent business results.
* Measurement remains incomplete and should be interpreted carefully.
* Strategy should connect experiments with customer behavior and revenue.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS teams should avoid rebuilding their complete marketing strategy around an unverified AI-search tactic.

A better approach is to:

* Preserve strong SEO foundations
* Run controlled GEO experiments
* Document assumptions
* Measure citations and visibility
* Track pipeline and revenue
* Compare results against existing channels
* Stop activities that do not produce useful outcomes

## Source 2 — The GEO Gold Rush Problem

* **Published:** April 8, 2026
* **Author and host:** Alex Birkett
* **Format:** Podcast
* **URL:** `https://beomniscient.com/blog/the-geo-gold-rush-problem/`
* **Primary topic:** AI-search hype, flawed attribution, brand sentiment, and off-page signals

### Summary

The discussion warns that AI search is experiencing a gold-rush period in which marketers promote spam tactics, weak case studies, and unreliable attribution models.

Many teams optimize for visibility or citations without connecting those metrics to revenue, pipeline, or customer outcomes.

The episode argues that visibility is only the first step. Companies also need to understand how AI systems describe the brand, what sentiment they communicate, and whether the positioning supports the company’s actual go-to-market strategy.

Customer research, reviews, PR, community discussions, and trusted third-party sources can strongly influence AI-generated descriptions and recommendations.

### Key Insights

* The SEO-versus-AEO debate often distracts from business value.
* Visibility and citation counts are insufficient without pipeline or revenue context.
* AI-search attribution remains unreliable.
* Large datasets can still be interpreted to support weak conclusions.
* Customer research remains more valuable than unsupported inference.
* Brand sentiment and positioning influence AI-generated answers.
* Reviews, PR, communities, and off-page sources shape AI visibility.
* Authentic engagement is more sustainable than manipulation.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should monitor:

* How AI systems describe their products
* Whether the company is included in relevant shortlists
* The sentiment of product recommendations
* Review-site categorization
* Customer feedback
* Community discussions
* Competitor comparisons
* The relationship between visibility and revenue

## Source 3 — Where the Puck Is Going in AI Search

* **Published:** April 3, 2026
* **Author:** Alex Birkett
* **Format:** Article
* **URL:** `https://beomniscient.com/blog/where-the-puck-is-going-in-ai-search/`
* **Primary topic:** Strategic experimentation, changing platform incentives, and sustainable organic growth

### Summary

Alex argues that AI-search strategy cannot be developed in isolation.

Marketers operate within a system containing customers, competitors, search platforms, AI products, communities, publishers, and other parties with different incentives.

A tactic that works temporarily may lose effectiveness when competitors copy it or platforms change their systems. Therefore, companies should avoid building their entire strategy around a short-term loophole.

The stronger approach is to invest in durable assets: customer knowledge, brand authority, product expertise, trusted distribution, strong content, and experimentation capabilities.

### Key Insights

* Customer, competitor, and platform behavior changes continuously.
* Short-term tactics lose value when they become widely copied.
* Companies should consider incentives and competitive responses.
* Sustainable strategies rely on assets that are difficult to replicate.
* Experimentation should support learning rather than only producing vanity metrics.
* Brand, product, content, and distribution should work as one system.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should prioritize durable advantages such as:

* Proprietary product knowledge
* Customer research
* Original data
* Case studies
* Brand credibility
* Strong community relationships
* High-quality educational resources
* Reliable technical documentation
* Repeatable experimentation systems

## Consolidated Research Takeaways

Alex Birkett’s work suggests that B2B SaaS teams should:

* Avoid both AI-search denial and AI-search hype
* Test new tactics with clear hypotheses
* Connect visibility metrics with revenue and pipeline
* Treat attribution claims cautiously
* Conduct direct customer research
* Monitor brand sentiment and positioning
* Strengthen off-page authority through reviews, PR, and communities
* Build durable content and brand assets
* Use experimentation to learn rather than chase vanity metrics

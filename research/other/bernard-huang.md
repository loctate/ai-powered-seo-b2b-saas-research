## Source 1 — The 2026 SEO Playbook: How AI Is Reshaping Search

* **Published:** November 24, 2025
* **Author:** Bernard Huang
* **Format:** Article
* **URL:** `https://www.clearscope.io/blog/2026-seo-aeo-playbook`
* **Primary topic:** How brands can remain visible across traditional and AI-powered search environments

### Summary

Bernard explains that search visibility now operates across two connected environments.

Traditional search presents ranked links and encourages users to visit websites. AI-powered search retrieves information from multiple sources, reasons across those sources, and presents synthesized answers directly to users.

Brands therefore need to perform well in both environments. Traditional SEO remains valuable, but organizations must also understand whether their content, expertise, and brand are being recognized inside AI-generated responses.

The article emphasizes that AI visibility should be treated as an ongoing process. Companies need to identify the topics they want to be associated with, improve the clarity and credibility of their information, monitor where they are mentioned or cited, and continue strengthening those signals.

### Key Insights

* Traditional search and AI-powered search will coexist.
* Rankings alone no longer represent complete search visibility.
* Companies should measure whether their expertise appears within AI-generated answers.
* Content needs clear context, credible information, and strong topic coverage.
* AI-search optimization is an iterative process rather than a one-time technical task.
* Brand recognition and topical authority influence modern discovery.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should monitor whether AI systems:

* Recognize the company within its product category
* Explain the product accurately
* Associate it with the correct use cases
* Include it in relevant software comparisons
* Cite its documentation or educational content
* Recommend it to appropriate customer segments

Visibility measurement should combine traditional SEO data with AI mentions, citations, brand descriptions, referral traffic, and recommendation presence.

## Source 2 — The New Frontier of SEO: Why AI Optimization Is About Intelligence, Not Automation

* **Published:** November 25, 2025
* **Author:** Bernard Huang
* **Format:** Article
* **URL:** `https://www.clearscope.io/blog/seo-ai-optimization-is-about-intelligence`
* **Primary topic:** Using AI to amplify insight rather than produce generic content at scale

### Summary

Bernard argues that the future of SEO will not be won through indiscriminate automation.

AI can lower the cost of producing content, but inexpensive production does not automatically create useful knowledge, authority, or differentiation.

As AI systems mediate more of the discovery process, visibility increasingly depends on whether a brand contributes explanations, definitions, examples, and expertise that can be incorporated into generated answers.

The strongest companies will use AI to support research and execution while continuing to invest in original knowledge, subject-matter expertise, product insight, and clear strategic thinking.

### Key Insights

* Automation is easy to replicate and therefore provides limited differentiation.
* Human insight and original knowledge remain scarce and valuable.
* AI should amplify expertise instead of replacing it.
* Brands should build connected information ecosystems, not isolated pages.
* Recognition within AI systems may become more important than a single ranking position.
* Generic AI-generated content is unlikely to create durable authority.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

A B2B SaaS content operation should use AI to accelerate:

* Research organization
* Content briefing
* Outlining
* Editing
* Repurposing
* Internal linking
* Metadata preparation
* Content updating

Human specialists should remain responsible for:

* Customer insight
* Product accuracy
* Original opinions
* Case studies
* Proprietary data
* Strategic positioning
* Final editorial approval

## Source 3 — How AEO / GEO / AI SEO Actually Works

* **Published:** December 11, 2025
* **Presenter:** Bernard Huang
* **Format:** Webinar
* **URL:** `https://www.clearscope.io/webinars/how-aeo-geo-actually-works`
* **Primary topic:** Building visibility across Google, Gemini, ChatGPT, and emerging AI-search platforms

### Summary

This webinar presents a practical framework for understanding AEO, GEO, and AI SEO.

The central focus is not simply generating more content. It is improving the ability of search and AI platforms to discover, understand, trust, retrieve, and present a brand’s information.

The framework connects traditional SEO principles with AI visibility, topic selection, content quality, source credibility, brand recognition, and ongoing measurement.

### Key Insights

* AI-search optimization builds on established SEO foundations.
* Content should be easy to understand, retrieve, and quote.
* Clear topic ownership strengthens brand recognition.
* AI visibility should be measured across multiple platforms.
* Companies need to understand both citations and non-click visibility.
* Human expertise remains necessary to guide content strategy.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should develop authoritative information around:

* Product categories
* Customer problems
* Product use cases
* Integrations
* Comparisons
* Implementation requirements
* Security and compliance
* Customer outcomes
* Industry terminology

The objective is to become a trusted source that AI systems can use when explaining the market and recommending solutions.

## Consolidated Research Takeaways

Bernard Huang’s work suggests that B2B SaaS teams should:

* Optimize for both traditional search and AI-generated answers
* Measure recognition and citations alongside rankings and clicks
* Use AI to amplify human knowledge rather than create generic content
* Build topic authority around products and customer problems
* Create clear, credible, and interconnected information
* Maintain human ownership of expertise, strategy, and quality
* Treat AI visibility as an ongoing measurement and improvement process

## Source 1 — How AI Search Is Rewiring B2B Software Buying

* **Published:** April 20, 2026
* **Author:** Ross Simmonds
* **Format:** Research commentary
* **URL:** https://foundationinc.co/lab/g2-answer-economy-report/
* **Primary topic:** How AI platforms influence B2B software research, evaluation, and vendor selection

### Summary

Ross Simmonds reviews G2 research describing how AI search is changing the B2B software buying process.

In the traditional search journey, buyers collected information from several websites, compared alternatives, and formed their own conclusions. In an AI-assisted journey, the platform may retrieve information, evaluate sources, summarize the findings, and present a shortlist before the buyer visits a vendor’s website.

This changes the role of content. A software company may be evaluated before it receives a website visit, form submission, or demo request. Brand information available across review platforms, third-party publications, communities, and company-owned pages can influence whether the vendor is included in an AI-generated answer.

### Key Insights

* B2B buyers increasingly use AI systems to perform early-stage research.
* AI platforms can synthesize information and recommend a shortlist of vendors.
* A company may influence a purchase decision before receiving direct website traffic.
* Visibility depends on the wider information ecosystem, not only the company website.
* Reviews, comparisons, community discussions, and third-party references can shape AI recommendations.
* Traditional last-click attribution may not capture the full influence of AI discovery.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should ensure that accurate product information is available across:

* Product and solution pages
* Review websites
* Comparison pages
* Customer case studies
* Industry publications
* Community discussions
* Integration directories
* Technical documentation
* Independent expert content

The company should also monitor how AI platforms describe its product, competitors, category, strengths, weaknesses, pricing context, and target users.

## Source 2 — Google’s Preferred Sources Are Officially Live: The Marketing Impact

* **Published:** May 5, 2026
* **Author:** Ross Simmonds
* **Format:** Article
* **URL:** https://foundationinc.co/lab/googles-preferred-sources
* **Primary topic:** Search personalization, source preference, content distribution, and multi-surface visibility

### Summary

Ross explains that Google’s Preferred Sources feature allows users to indicate which publications they trust and want to see more frequently.

He uses this development to illustrate a broader shift: search is no longer represented only by a fixed list of ranked webpages. Users may encounter AI Overviews, AI Mode, Top Stories, Discover, video results, Reddit discussions, shopping results, maps, and knowledge panels.

Each surface may use different signals. Therefore, optimizing one webpage for one keyword is no longer sufficient to build complete search visibility.

### Key Insights

* Search personalization is moving from query-level signals toward source-level preferences.
* Search results are divided across multiple discovery surfaces.
* Different surfaces reward different content formats and authority signals.
* Brands need to be mentioned, cited, selected, and trusted across multiple platforms.
* Content distribution is becoming closely connected to search optimization.
* Audience trust and repeated exposure can create compounding visibility.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

A B2B SaaS company should not depend only on publishing blog articles and waiting for organic rankings.

It should distribute useful material through:

* LinkedIn
* YouTube
* Reddit and relevant communities
* Newsletters
* Partner websites
* Industry publications
* Product directories
* Review platforms
* Webinars and podcasts

A strong AI-powered SEO strategy should combine content creation, distribution, third-party authority, brand recognition, and customer trust.

## Consolidated Research Takeaways

Ross Simmonds’ work suggests that B2B SaaS content teams should:

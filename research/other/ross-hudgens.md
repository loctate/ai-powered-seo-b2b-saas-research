## Source 1 — How GEO Actually Works with Bernard Huang

* **Published:** January 12, 2026
* **Host:** Ross Hudgens
* **Guest:** Bernard Huang
* **Format:** Podcast and video discussion
* **URL:** https://www.siegemedia.com/conversation/how-geo-actually-works-w-bernard-huang
* **Primary topic:** The relationship between SEO, GEO, AI retrieval, mentions, citations, and measurement

### Summary

Ross Hudgens and Bernard Huang discuss whether Generative Engine Optimization is a new discipline or an evolution of established SEO principles.

The conversation covers how AI Mode and AI Overviews affect search behavior, how AI systems differ from keyword-based search, and why traditional top-of-funnel traffic may decline.

They also explore the growing importance of brand mentions, citations, impressions, and branded search rather than relying only on backlinks and organic clicks.

### Key Insights

* Strong SEO foundations still contribute to GEO performance.
* AI systems retrieve and synthesize information differently from traditional ranked results.
* Top-of-funnel website traffic may decline as platforms answer more questions directly.
* Brand mentions may become more important alongside backlinks.
* AI-search success cannot be measured only through clicks.
* Citations, impressions, brand search, and recommendation visibility are useful supporting indicators.
* Content teams must prepare for a much longer and more varied query tail.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should expand measurement beyond keyword rankings and organic sessions.

A broader dashboard may include:

* AI platform referrals
* Brand mentions
* Citations
* Branded search growth
* Share of recommendation
* Product-category visibility
* Assisted conversions
* Demo requests influenced by AI research
* Competitor visibility comparisons

## Source 2 — What Your CMO Needs to Know About AI Search

* **Published:** June 15, 2026
* **Host:** Ross Hudgens
* **Guest:** Tom Critchlow
* **Format:** Podcast and video discussion
* **URL:** https://www.siegemedia.com/conversation/what-your-cmo-needs-to-know-about-ai-search-w-tom-critchlow
* **Primary topic:** Executive understanding of GEO, AI-search measurement, brand awareness, and organizational strategy

### Summary

Ross and Tom Critchlow discuss what GEO means for marketing leaders and why the field remains difficult to measure precisely.

The discussion suggests that AI-search measurement may resemble brand measurement more than traditional SEO reporting. The important question is not only whether an AI system cites a company, but also whether the model recognizes, describes, and recommends the brand accurately.

This shifts attention from monitoring individual prompts toward broader indicators such as brand awareness, share of model, category association, sentiment, and competitive positioning.

### Key Insights

* GEO is still evolving and does not yet have a universally accepted measurement framework.
* Prompt tracking alone may provide an incomplete picture.
* AI-search visibility is connected to brand awareness and category recognition.
* Companies should examine how models describe them, not only whether they receive a citation.
* Executive teams need realistic expectations about attribution.
* AI-search strategy requires coordination across SEO, brand, PR, content, and leadership.
* Measurement should combine quantitative and qualitative evidence.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

For B2B SaaS companies, the AI-search question is broader than traffic.

Teams should evaluate:

* Is the brand included in relevant vendor shortlists?
* Is the product associated with the correct use cases?
* Are its strengths and limitations described accurately?
* Is it compared fairly with competitors?
* Are the correct customer segments being recommended?
* Does AI-generated information match the company’s current product?

## Consolidated Research Takeaways

Ross Hudgens’ material suggests that a B2B SaaS GEO program should:

* Build on strong SEO and content foundations
* Measure citations and mentions alongside traffic
* Treat brand perception as part of AI-search performance
* Monitor how AI models describe and recommend the company
* Combine quantitative and qualitative analysis
* Coordinate SEO, content, PR, brand, and executive stakeholders
* Avoid promising precise attribution where reliable data does not exist

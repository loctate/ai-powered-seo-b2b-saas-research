## Source 1 — Google’s Guidance on AI Search Is Naive and Self-Serving

* **Published:** May 18, 2026
* **Author:** Mike King
* **Format:** Technical article
* **URL:** `https://ipullrank.com/google-ai-search-guidance`
* **Primary topic:** Why AI-search optimization requires a broader multi-platform perspective

### Summary

Mike argues that Google’s public guidance represents only one view of AI-search optimization.

Different platforms use different infrastructures, retrieval systems, ranking processes, incentives, and content-selection methods. A strategy designed only around Google’s recommendations may therefore fail to account for how ChatGPT, Bing, Perplexity, Anthropic, and other systems process information.

The article emphasizes passage retrieval and content chunking. Even when platforms claim that special formatting is unnecessary, AI systems still retrieve and compare individual content passages.

Organizations should therefore create content sections that preserve meaning, contain clear claims, and remain understandable when separated from the surrounding page.

### Key Insights

* Google’s recommendations should not be treated as universal rules.
* AI-search platforms use different technical systems and incentives.
* Passage-level retrieval affects how content is selected.
* Content chunks should preserve meaning and factual claims.
* Multi-platform optimization requires broader technical knowledge.
* SEO is evolving toward content engineering and Relevance Engineering.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS content should contain clear standalone explanations of:

* Product capabilities
* Integrations
* Use cases
* Limitations
* Pricing context
* Security features
* Implementation requirements
* Customer outcomes

Each section should remain understandable when retrieved independently by an AI system.

## Source 2 — Beyond RAG: Why Every AI Search Platform Is Now Agentic

* **Published:** May 20, 2026
* **Author:** Mike King
* **Format:** Technical article
* **URL:** `https://ipullrank.com/agentic-rag`
* **Primary topic:** Agentic AI-search systems and their implications for content strategy

### Summary

Mike explains that AI-search systems are evolving beyond simple retrieval-augmented generation.

Agentic systems can decompose a user request, create additional searches, evaluate intermediate results, select tools, retrieve several sources, and refine an answer before presenting it.

This creates a more complex discovery process. A single user prompt may generate multiple hidden queries, and content may need to compete for inclusion at several stages of the answer-generation pipeline.

### Key Insights

* AI-search systems increasingly perform multiple reasoning and retrieval steps.
* One user prompt may produce many hidden or synthetic queries.
* Content visibility depends on relevance across a broader query space.
* AI systems may retrieve small passages rather than complete pages.
* Clear facts, dates, entities, relationships, and definitions improve extractability.
* Content teams should prepare for machine-led research journeys.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

A B2B SaaS buyer may ask one complex question that causes an AI platform to investigate:

* Product category
* Pricing
* Integrations
* Security
* Reviews
* Alternatives
* Implementation
* Suitability for a specific company size
* Customer sentiment

The SaaS company should provide clear and consistent information covering each of these subtopics.

## Source 3 — How to Win Visibility in AI Search: The AI Search Manual Walkthrough

* **Publication date:** Not displayed on the source page
* **Presenter:** Mike King
* **Format:** Webinar
* **URL:** `https://ipullrank.com/resources/webinars/ai-search-manual-webinar`
* **Primary topic:** Retrieval, embeddings, query fan-out, extractable content, and AI-search measurement

### Summary

The webinar explains AI search through the framework of Relevance Engineering.

Mike contrasts deterministic keyword-based rankings with probabilistic AI-generated answers. AI systems use retrieval, embeddings, semantic similarity, synthetic queries, and passage selection to decide which information may be included.

The session also explains query fan-out, where one prompt generates several related searches. It recommends building content that is specific, extractable, semantically clear, and supported by dates, evidence, entities, and structured claims.

### Key Insights

* AI-generated answers are probabilistic rather than fixed.
* Query fan-out expands one prompt into several searches.
* Passage relevance matters more than only page-level relevance.
* Short and specific content sections are easier to retrieve.
* Dates, data, definitions, and entity relationships strengthen extractability.
* AI-search reporting requires new visibility and citation metrics.
* Multimodal content can improve representation across AI-search surfaces.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should build interconnected content across:

* Product pages
* Educational articles
* Documentation
* Comparison pages
* Videos
* Webinars
* Images and diagrams
* Customer case studies
* Third-party publications

The goal is not only to rank one page. It is to create an information ecosystem from which AI systems can retrieve accurate product knowledge.

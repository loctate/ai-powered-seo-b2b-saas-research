## Post 1 — AI Content Was Not Good Enough. Now It Is.

* **Published:** March 16, 2026
* **URL:** `https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_ai-content-wasnt-good-enough-now-it-is-activity-7439350229327302656-3JNC`
* **Topic:** Improvements in AI-generated content quality

### Summary

Ryan argues that generative AI has reached a level where it can produce content comparable to much of the conventional SEO content historically created by human marketing teams.

He describes AI as becoming more capable at research, following brand guidelines, responding to feedback, and producing content efficiently.

Instead of denying these improvements, Ryan recommends that marketers focus more attention on work where human contribution remains highly differentiated.

### Key Insights

* AI content quality is improving rapidly.
* AI can follow documented brand and editorial guidelines.
* Routine SEO content is increasingly suitable for automation.
* Content teams need to redefine where human work creates the greatest value.
* Original research, judgment, positioning, and thought leadership remain important.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies can automate repeatable production tasks while directing human specialists toward customer research, product expertise, differentiated opinions, case studies, and strategic decision-making.

## Post 2 — AI Content Automation System Walkthrough

* **Published:** May 26, 2026
* **URL:** `https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_heres-a-full-9-minute-walkthrough-of-my-activity-7464941601384128512-4-xC`
* **Topic:** End-to-end AI content workflow

### Summary

Ryan demonstrates a no-code AI content system that uses Ahrefs data and reusable skill files based on his editorial process.

The workflow includes keyword research, competitor analysis, topic research, outlining, product references, drafting, internal linking, basic image generation, editing, publication formatting, and post-publication content updates.

Ryan also makes clear that the workflow should not operate without human oversight.

### Workflow Components

* Keyword research
* Competitor analysis
* Topic research
* Content outlining
* Product and article references
* Draft creation
* Internal linking
* Basic image generation
* Content preview
* Human editing
* Publication formatting
* Content updating

### Key Insights

* Editorial expertise can be converted into reusable AI instructions.
* AI workflows can support the full content lifecycle.
* Access to reliable company and SEO data improves the workflow.
* No-code tools can make sophisticated automation accessible.
* Human review remains necessary before publication.
* Content updates should be part of the system, not treated as an afterthought.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

This post provides a direct model for a B2B SaaS content operation. Product documentation, approved claims, internal links, brand guidelines, and editorial procedures could be connected to an AI-assisted production system.

The strongest model is not one-click publishing. It is a controlled workflow in which AI performs repeatable tasks while human editors approve strategy, facts, product references, and final quality.

## Post 3 — Ahrefs AI Search Benchmark Research

* **Published:** May 27, 2026
* **URL:** `https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_ahrefs-ai-search-benchmark-report-q1-2026-activity-7465398165567959040-J1GF`
* **Topic:** AI-search research and data studies

### Summary

Ryan highlights a consolidated collection of Ahrefs research on AI search. The related benchmark combines more than 100 million data points, 13 studies, and contributions from eight authors.

The studies examine systems including Google AI Overviews, AI Mode, and ChatGPT, providing a data-based alternative to unsupported assumptions about AI-search performance.

### Key Insights

* AI-search strategy should be informed by data rather than speculation.
* Different AI-search platforms should be analyzed separately.
* Large datasets can reveal patterns that individual examples cannot.
* Benchmarking should be repeated as platforms change.
* Research should examine citations, sources, visibility, errors, and user outcomes.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS teams should create measurement systems before scaling AI-search initiatives. Useful indicators may include AI referrals, source citations, brand mentions, competitive visibility, influenced conversions, and changes in product discovery.

## Consolidated Research Takeaways

Ryan Law’s LinkedIn content suggests that an AI-powered SaaS content operation should:

* Automate repeatable production tasks

* Focus human effort on differentiated and original work
* Include content updating in the production system
* Use empirical AI-search research instead of unsupported claims

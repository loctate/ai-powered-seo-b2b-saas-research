## Post 2 — SEO in 2026: Higher Standards and Technical Gaps

* **Published:** April 7, 2026
* **URL:** `https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_seo-in-2026-higher-standards-ai-influence-activity-7447220940360511488-oKW0`
* **Topic:** Technical SEO foundations in an AI-influenced search environment

### Summary

Aleyda highlights research showing that technical web standards are improving, but many websites still have gaps that limit search visibility.

AI search introduces another discovery layer, but technical fundamentals such as crawlability, indexing, HTTPS, canonicalization, error management, and performance remain important.

### Key Insights

* AI search does not remove the need for technical SEO.
* Small technical issues can collectively reduce visibility.
* Crawlability and indexability remain foundational.
* New AI-search tactics should be built on reliable website infrastructure.
* Search teams should not ignore established technical standards while pursuing new trends.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS websites often contain JavaScript applications, product pages, documentation, integrations, help centers, and international content.

Technical teams should ensure that important information is crawlable, indexable, correctly canonicalized, and accessible without unnecessary rendering barriers.

## Post 3 — Updated AI Search Optimization Checklist

* **Published:** May 29, 2026
* **URL:** `https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleyda_ive-just-updated-my-ai-search-optimization-activity-7466150460299857921-664d`
* **Topic:** Strategic AI-search optimization workflow

### Summary

Aleyda presents an updated AI Search Optimization Checklist and worksheet containing 12 practical steps.

The workflow begins by identifying which AI-assisted journeys matter, measuring where a brand is visible or absent, identifying the owned and third-party sources influencing answers, diagnosing gaps, prioritizing improvements, validating results, and reporting progress without overstating impact.

The updated framework also covers prompt measurement, source ecosystems, commercial readiness, localization, and different levels of reporting confidence.

### Key Insights

* AI-search optimization should begin with relevant customer journeys.
* Brands should identify where they are mentioned, cited, recommended, or missing.
* Both owned pages and third-party sources influence AI answers.
* Gaps may involve content, accessibility, entity clarity, localization, or commercial information.
* Measurement should distinguish observed, proxy, and modeled signals.
* Changes should be validated through a recurring process.
* Reporting should avoid unsupported attribution claims.
* International and local contexts require separate consideration.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should map prompts and journeys across awareness, evaluation, comparison, purchase, implementation, and support.

They should identify which sources influence AI-generated product recommendations and improve both owned content and external brand representation.

Commercial information—including pricing context, features, integrations, limitations, implementation requirements, and use cases—should be clear enough for customers and AI systems to understand accurately.

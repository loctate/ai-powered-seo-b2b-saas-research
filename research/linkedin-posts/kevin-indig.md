## Post 1 — AI SEO Adoption Is a Change-Management Challenge

* **Published:** February 26, 2026
* **URL:** `https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_want-an-8x-success-multiplier-on-your-ai-activity-7432767721030754306-iorn`
* **Topic:** Organizational adoption of AI SEO

### Summary

Kevin argues that successful AI SEO adoption is not primarily determined by finding another tool. It depends on how effectively leaders guide their organizations through uncertainty and operational change.

Many teams approach AI search as a binary situation in which they either understand everything or are failing. Kevin instead presents AI SEO as an educational and organizational transition.

He emphasizes that leaders should communicate honestly about what is not yet measurable while still acting on clear risks, such as a brand being absent from important AI-generated answers.

### Key Insights

* AI-search adoption requires education and internal alignment.
* Leaders should acknowledge uncertainty rather than overstate confidence.
* Organizations can begin acting before every measurement problem has been solved.
* Change management is part of AI SEO implementation.
* AI-search initiatives require support from stakeholders beyond the SEO team.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies often involve several stakeholders in content, product, sales, brand, and demand generation. An AI SEO strategy therefore needs internal communication, shared expectations, and gradual adoption instead of being treated as a standalone technical project.

## Post 2 — Search Discovery Is Expanding Beyond Google

* **Published:** March 17, 2026
* **URL:** `https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_as-googles-ai-overviews-begin-to-impact-activity-7439645486552358912-bPvT`
* **Topic:** Search diversification and emerging discovery platforms

### Summary

Kevin explains that search behavior is expanding across AI tools, communities, marketplaces, video platforms, and alternative search environments.

Companies that continue allocating most of their search investment only to Google risk over-optimizing for one channel. Defending branded keywords in traditional search does not automatically create visibility on AI platforms or community-driven discovery channels.

### Key Insights

* Search discovery is becoming fragmented across multiple platforms.
* Google remains important but should not be the only visibility channel.
* Reddit, Amazon, YouTube, AI assistants, and other platforms influence early research.
* Brand visibility must be developed across the broader discovery ecosystem.
* Search, content distribution, paid media, and community strategy are becoming more connected.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS buyers may discover products through Google, YouTube, LinkedIn, communities, review platforms, Reddit discussions, and AI-generated recommendations.

A SaaS content strategy should therefore repurpose and distribute useful material across the channels where buyers research problems, vendors, and product alternatives.

## Post 3 — SEO, Marketing, and Technology Predictions for 2026

* **Published:** December 2, 2025
* **URL:** `https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_my-2026-predictions-just-went-out-to-23k-activity-7401658484473176064-RKiC`
* **Topic:** AI visibility, search quality, advertising, and platform change

### Summary

Kevin shares several predictions for 2026, including greater scrutiny of AI visibility tools, future quality updates for AI platforms, declining clicks from traditional search, increasing restrictions on AI crawlers, and the development of advertising systems inside AI products.

The post illustrates how search, AI visibility, publisher economics, advertising, and platform governance are becoming interconnected.

### Key Insights

* AI visibility tools will face stronger demands for reliable measurement.
* AI platforms will need quality-control systems.
* Declining search clicks may cause publishers to restrict crawler access.
* AI-generated and human-created information ecosystems may become more separated.
* Advertising inside AI platforms may create new demand and audience data.

### Relevance to B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS teams should avoid relying on one AI visibility score. They should combine referral traffic, citations, branded searches, assisted conversions, pipeline influence, and qualitative presence analysis.

The predictions also reinforce the importance of creating original information that customers and platforms cannot easily obtain from generic content.
